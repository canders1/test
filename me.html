About

I am about to graduate with a PhD in Linguistics from UMass Amherst. In June 2020, I defended my dissertation, entitled Shifting the Perspectival Landscape.

In Fall 2021, I will be starting as an assistant professor in the Wellesley College Computer Science department.

I study perspective in language: the ways in which people's use of language depends on their point-of-view. This includes context-sensitive expressions that describe space and time as well as the ways in which people express their beliefs and opinions.

My work uses experimental and computational methods to understand how people express and track perspective across discourses and conversations. I use Bayesian inference techniques to model reasoning about perspective in conversation, and construct web-based experiments to gather empirical evidence.

I graduated from Swarthmore College in 2014 with a major in Linguistics and a minor in Computer Science.

I have also been affiliated with the Speech and Language group at BBN Technologies, where I worked on several topics in automatic speech recognition.
